Three hard problems with siloed industrial data

Manufacturing facilities generate more data than almost any other industry — a single production line can produce terabytes of sensor data per day. But data volume is not the problem. These three problems are:

Problem 1: OT data and IT data speak different languages

Operational technology (OT) — SCADA, PLCs, sensors — produces time-series data at millisecond resolution in historians like OSIsoft PI or InfluxDB. Information technology (IT) — ERP, MES, CMMS — stores transactional data in relational databases like SAP or Oracle. These two worlds were never designed to talk to each other. The vibration sensor on Bearing 7 doesn't know which production order was running when it spiked. The ERP doesn't know that Line 3 was running 15% above rated speed when the defect rate tripled. Databricks' manufacturing page talks about a "Data Intelligence Platform for Manufacturing" — but that platform still requires a data engineering team to build the OT-IT bridge. InfiniSynapse connects to both worlds directly and joins them on timestamps and asset IDs automatically.

Problem 2: Predictive maintenance requires cross-source analysis that no single system can do

Predicting a failure requires joining at least four data streams: (1) sensor trends showing degradation patterns, (2) maintenance history showing what was replaced when, (3) production schedules showing how hard the asset was run, and (4) quality records showing whether output was affected. Each stream lives in a separate system. The CMMS tells you when maintenance was done. The SCADA historian tells you what the asset's condition is. The MES tells you the production load. The QMS tells you the quality outcome. Without joining these, "predictive maintenance" is just "sensor threshold alerting" — which generates so many false positives that maintenance teams ignore them. InfiniSynapse's multi-source federation is what turns alerting into actual prediction.

Problem 3: Process optimization is limited by data access speed

When a process engineer wants to investigate why defect rates spiked on Line 3 during the night shift last Tuesday, they need data from: the MES (production counts, speeds, operators on shift), the QMS (defect measurements by batch), the SCADA historian (temperature, pressure, RPM during those batches), and the ERP (which material supplier's batch was being used). Getting all of that data typically requires filing requests with 3 different departments, waiting days for exports, and then spending hours in Excel. By the time the analysis is done, 3 more days of production have passed. Databricks highlights the need to "optimize manufacturing processes" — but optimization velocity is gated by data access, not analytics capability. InfiniSynapse removes the data access bottleneck.

Six core manufacturing analytics workflows

These are the six highest-frequency manufacturing analytics workflows process engineers, plant managers, and operations leaders run on InfiniSynapse:

1. Predictive maintenance and failure forecasting — predictive maintenance

Ask: "Which assets across all lines show sensor readings trending toward known failure thresholds over the last 7 days? Rank by risk score: rate of change × proximity to threshold × days since last maintenance. Cross-reference with the maintenance schedule — which high-risk assets have no planned maintenance in the next 14 days?" InfiniSynapse queries vibration, temperature, and pressure trends from the SCADA historian, compares against failure thresholds from your Knowledge Base, and checks the CMMS for maintenance records. The output is a prioritized work list for the maintenance team — not a generic alert, but a ranked, context-aware prediction. Databricks highlights the need to "mitigate equipment failures, reducing downtime" — this is the workflow that delivers it.

2. Process parameter optimization — process optimization

Ask: "For Line 3 producing SKU-4421, identify which process parameters (temperature, pressure, line speed, material feed rate) correlate most strongly with first-pass yield. What are the optimal ranges for the top 3 parameters?" InfiniSynapse joins quality records from the QMS with process parameters from the SCADA historian and MES, correlates each parameter against yield outcomes, and returns the optimal parameter ranges. This is the analysis that traditionally requires a Six Sigma Black Belt and 2 weeks of Minitab work — now a 2-minute query.

3. OEE analysis across lines, shifts, and products — OEE analysis

Ask: "Calculate OEE (Availability × Performance × Quality) for all 6 production lines, broken down by shift and by SKU, for the last 30 days. Which shift-SKU combinations have the lowest Performance component? What are the top 3 downtime reasons for those combinations?" InfiniSynapse pulls availability data from MES (planned vs. actual run time), performance data from MES (ideal cycle time × total units ÷ run time), and quality data from QMS (good units ÷ total units). The OEE formula is stored in your Knowledge Base and applied consistently across all analyses.

4. Digital supply chain and inventory optimization — digital supply chain

Ask: "For the next 30 days of production schedule, flag any materials where current inventory + inbound POs covers less than 120% of planned consumption. Rank by production impact: which shortages would stop the highest-revenue production lines?" InfiniSynapse joins production schedules from MES, inventory levels from ERP, open purchase orders from ERP, and supplier lead times from the procurement system. Databricks emphasizes the need to "leverage real-time insights to anticipate demand, streamline logistics" for digital supply chain — InfiniSynapse delivers this by connecting the data that already exists in your systems.

5. Quality analytics and root cause investigation — quality analytics

Ask: "When defect rate on Line 2 exceeded 2.5% in the last quarter, what were the common factors? Analyze by material batch, shift, operator, ambient temperature, and production speed. Which factor explains the most variance?" InfiniSynapse joins defect records from QMS with material batch data from ERP, shift logs from MES, sensor data from SCADA, and environmental readings from IoT. This is a multi-source root cause analysis that would normally take a quality engineer a full week — now a single query.

6. Energy consumption and sustainability analytics — industrial AI / IoT analytics

Ask: "Compare energy consumption (kWh per unit produced) across all lines for the last 6 months. Which lines have a statistically significant upward trend? Correlate with production speed and maintenance events — is increased energy consumption predicting upcoming failures?" This connects energy meter IoT data with production counts from MES and maintenance records from CMMS. Databricks frames this as "Industrial AI: optimize processes for safer, more efficient operations" — energy efficiency is one of the highest-ROI applications of industrial AI, and it requires joining data from systems that were never integrated.
