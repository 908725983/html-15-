Three hard problems with financial data analytics

Financial institutions don't lack data — they lack secure, connected, and accessible data analytics. These three problems are unique to regulated financial services:

Problem 1: Financial data is locked in silos — and for good regulatory reasons

Core banking, risk management, compliance, treasury, and customer systems are intentionally separated for security and regulatory reasons. But this separation makes cross-system analysis — the kind needed for risk aggregation, fraud detection, and regulatory reporting — painfully manual. A risk analyst asking "what's our aggregate exposure to commercial real estate across all lending books?" must pull data from multiple loan systems (commercial, CRE, construction), manually normalize exposure definitions, and hope nothing was missed. Databricks's financial services positioning — "reduce risks using predictive insights" — assumes the data unification problem is already solved. For most financial institutions, it isn't. InfiniSynapse solves it at the query layer without requiring a full data platform migration.

Problem 2: Most AI analytics tools are SaaS-only — and financial institutions cannot use them

Julius AI, Hex, Powerdrill, and most modern AI analytics tools are SaaS-only. Using them means uploading financial data — transaction records, customer PII, risk models, portfolio positions — to external cloud servers. For regulated financial institutions, this is a non-starter. GDPR, SOX, PCI DSS, and national banking regulations impose strict data residency and processing requirements. This means most AI analytics innovation is simply unavailable to financial services teams. InfiniSynapse's Air-Gapped deployment was designed specifically for this constraint: all the AI analytics capability, none of the data leaving your controlled environment.

Problem 3: Fraud detection data is scattered across transaction systems, customer profiles, and external watchlists

Effective fraud detection requires joining transaction data (what happened), customer profile data (who did it), historical behavior patterns (is this normal for them?), and external watchlist data (are they on any sanctions or PEP lists?). These data sources live in different systems with different access controls. Most fraud analysts manually review transactions in one system, look up customers in another, and check watchlists in a third — a process that limits the number of transactions that can be reviewed and increases the chance of missing patterns that only emerge when data is joined. Databricks highlights "spot and block fraud with advanced analytics"; InfiniSynapse makes this cross-source fraud analysis self-service for fraud analysts — not just data scientists building ML models.

Five core financial services analytics workflows

These are the five highest-frequency financial services analytics workflows risk, compliance, fraud, and investment teams run on InfiniSynapse:

1. Fraud pattern detection and investigation — fraud prevention

Ask: "Identify transactions in the last 30 days that match suspicious patterns: unusually large amount relative to customer's 12-month average, first-time transfer to a new beneficiary in a high-risk jurisdiction, multiple transactions just below reporting thresholds within a 7-day window. Cross-reference with internal watchlist and recent SAR filings. Show customer profile summary for each flagged transaction." InfiniSynapse joins transaction data, customer profiles, historical behavior, and watchlist data — producing a prioritized investigation queue that a fraud analyst would normally spend days compiling. Databricks positions for "spot and block fraud with advanced analytics"; InfiniSynapse makes this investigative workflow self-service.

2. Risk exposure aggregation and limit monitoring — risk management

Ask: "Aggregate total credit exposure by sector, geography, and product type. Compare against internal risk limits and regulatory concentration limits. Flag any sector where exposure has grown more than 20% YoY or where current exposure exceeds 80% of the limit. Show exposure trend over the last 8 quarters." InfiniSynapse queries the loan book (core banking), risk-weighted asset calculations (risk engine), and limit framework (compliance) — joining on sector and product classifications. This is the analysis that risk managers currently produce quarterly with 2 weeks of data gathering; InfiniSynapse makes it a 2-minute daily check.

3. Regulatory compliance reporting — regulatory compliance / automated compliance reporting

Set up: "Generate the monthly compliance dashboard: capital adequacy ratios, liquidity coverage ratio, large exposure reporting, NPL ratio by segment, and any limit breaches or near-breaches. Include quarter-over-quarter trends and flag deteriorations." InfiniSynapse pulls data from core banking, risk, treasury, and compliance systems — applying your regulatory calculation methodology from the Knowledge Base — and produces the report. Databricks's financial services page emphasizes "automate reporting to simplify compliance"; InfiniSynapse delivers this without requiring data to move to a cloud platform.

4. Portfolio performance and investment analytics — investment analytics / portfolio analysis

Ask: "Analyze portfolio performance by asset class for the last quarter: total return, risk-adjusted return (Sharpe), sector allocation vs. benchmark, and top/bottom contributors to performance. Compare against the same quarter last year." InfiniSynapse queries portfolio management systems, market data feeds, and benchmark data — producing investment analytics that portfolio managers currently get from a combination of Bloomberg terminals, spreadsheet models, and data team reports.

5. Customer financial behavior and personalization — customer financial behavior / personalized offers

Ask: "Segment customers by financial behavior: transaction frequency, average balance, product holdings, life stage indicators (mortgage, student loans, retirement accounts). For each segment, identify: top cross-sell opportunities, churn risk indicators, and propensity for specific products based on behavior patterns of similar customers who already hold those products." InfiniSynapse joins transaction data, customer profiles, and product holdings — enabling the kind of customer intelligence that Databricks describes as "personalized offers" in financial services, but delivered through plain-English queries rather than ML model development.

When InfiniSynapse is not the right choice for financial services analytics

Honest framing — you likely don't need InfiniSynapse if:

  • You're building production financial ML systems. If you need to build, train, and deploy fraud detection ML models, credit scoring models, or algorithmic trading systems, you need a data science platform like Databricks or a dedicated ML infrastructure. InfiniSynapse is the analytics layer for business and risk teams, not a model development platform.
  • You already have a mature financial data platform with self-service BI. If your institution has invested in a modern data platform with governed financial data models and a BI layer that business users can access, InfiniSynapse may be additive rather than transformative. Its strongest value is institutions where financial data is still locked in operational silos.
  • Your analytics needs are single-system and well-defined. If every question you need answered lives in your core banking system's reporting module, and your regulatory reports are already automated, you're well-served. InfiniSynapse's value grows when questions span multiple financial systems that don't naturally connect.

InfiniSynapse creates the most value for financial institutions where cross-system analysis is currently manual, where data residency requirements prevent using SaaS AI tools, and where business and risk teams need self-service access to financial data without SQL or data engineering support.
