Three hard problems with siloed SaaS data

SaaS is a metrics-driven business, but the "single source of truth" is usually a spreadsheet that one person maintains. These three problems are why:

Problem 1: Billing data answers "how much," but not "why"

Stripe tells you MRR, upgrades, downgrades, and churn — the what. But it cannot tell you why a customer upgraded or churned. The "why" lives in product usage data (which features they used, how often they logged in, whether they invited teammates) and support data (how many tickets they filed, how fast they were resolved, what they complained about). Subscription analytics tools like ChartMogul and Baremetrics do an excellent job with the billing side, but they stop at the billing data boundary. InfiniSynapse crosses that boundary: join billing events with product usage patterns and support interactions to understand not just what happened to your MRR, but why.

Problem 2: LTV/CAC is the most important SaaS metric — and the hardest to calculate correctly

LTV requires: average revenue per account × gross margin ÷ churn rate. CAC requires: total sales + marketing spend ÷ new customers acquired. These sound simple. In practice: does "revenue" include discounts, credits, and one-time services? Does "churn" mean contract cancellation, non-renewal, or downgrade? Does "marketing spend" include content marketing salaries, conference sponsorships, and free-tier infrastructure costs? And which customers: all customers, or only those acquired in the last 12 months? Every SaaS company answers these differently — but within a company, the CEO, the VP of Sales, and the board need the same answers. InfiniSynapse's Knowledge Base stores your LTV/CAC formula once, and the AI applies it consistently across every analysis — eliminating the "your numbers look different from my numbers" conversation.

Problem 3: PLG funnels span 3-4 systems that were never integrated

A product-led growth funnel goes: visitor → signup (product DB) → activation (product DB: reached key milestone) → conversion to paid (Stripe) → expansion (Stripe: upgraded plan OR CRM: sales-assisted upsell). That's three systems — product DB, billing, CRM — with different notions of what a "customer" is. Most PLG companies cannot answer "what's our signup-to-paid conversion rate by acquisition channel?" without a data engineer building and maintaining a cross-source pipeline. InfiniSynapse was designed for exactly this: connect the three systems, define the funnel stages in the Knowledge Base, and ask the question in plain English.

Six core SaaS analytics workflows

These are the six highest-frequency SaaS analytics workflows founders, revenue operations, and product teams run on InfiniSynapse:

1. MRR/ARR growth decomposition — MRR tracking / ARR tracking

Ask: "Decompose MRR growth for the last 12 months into new, expansion, contraction, and churned components. Show as a waterfall by month. Which month had the highest gross churn, and which customers drove it?" InfiniSynapse queries subscription events from Stripe (new subscriptions, plan changes, cancellations), applies your MRR definitions from the Knowledge Base, and produces a complete growth decomposition — a report that typically takes a finance analyst 2 days in Excel. For SaaS companies tracking ARR, the same query works with annual contract values.

2. Net revenue retention by segment, plan, and cohort — subscription analytics

Ask: "Calculate NRR by customer segment (SMB, Mid-Market, Enterprise) and by plan tier for the last 4 quarters. For each segment, show the expansion rate and the churn rate separately. Which segment has NRR below 100% — and within that segment, what are the common characteristics of churned accounts?" InfiniSynapse joins billing data (revenue movements) with CRM data (segment, plan tier) and product usage data (engagement of churned accounts). NRR analysis that would take a week of SQL and cross-referencing is now a single query.

3. LTV/CAC by acquisition channel and cohort — LTV/CAC analysis

Ask: "Compare LTV, CAC, and LTV/CAC ratio by acquisition channel (paid search, organic, outbound sales, partner) for cohorts starting Q1 2025 through Q1 2026. Use a 12-month LTV window with gross margin at 75%. Which channel has a payback period over 18 months?" InfiniSynapse queries billing data for LTV (avg revenue × gross margin ÷ churn rate), CRM data for acquisition channel and CAC (sales + marketing spend by channel ÷ new customers), and your Knowledge Base for the formula parameters. This is the analysis that every SaaS board asks for and most companies spend 2 weeks preparing.

4. PLG funnel analytics: signup to expansion — usage analytics / PLG metrics

Ask: "Show the PLG funnel conversion rates for Q1: website visitor → signup → activated (reached key milestone within 7 days) → converted to paid → expanded (upgraded plan or added 2+ seats). Break down by acquisition channel. Where is the biggest conversion drop-off, and what product behaviors differentiate accounts that pass that stage from those that don't?" InfiniSynapse joins your product database (signup events, activation milestones, usage events), Stripe (conversion to paid, plan upgrades), and CRM (acquisition channel). This is the holy grail of PLG analytics — and it requires data from 3 separate systems that no single SaaS tool connects.

5. Churn prediction and reason analysis — churn analysis

Ask: "Identify accounts that churned in the last 90 days. For each, show: MRR lost, tenure at churn, support ticket volume in the 60 days before churn, product usage trend in the 30 days before churn (declining, flat, or increasing), and the primary reason cited in the cancellation survey or final support ticket." InfiniSynapse joins billing data (churn events), product usage data (engagement trend), and support tickets (cancellation reasons) — producing a churn post-mortem that connects the financial impact (MRR lost) with the behavioral leading indicators (usage decline) and the stated reasons (support feedback). This transforms churn from a number into something you can act on.

6. Feature-product usage correlation with retention — usage analytics / product analytics

Ask: "For accounts active in the last 12 months, correlate feature usage (which features, how frequently, by how many users) with account retention status at month 12. Which 5 features have the strongest positive correlation with retention? Which features are used by accounts that churned but almost never by accounts that renewed — indicating possible UX or value communication issues?" InfiniSynapse joins product usage events from your production DB with retention status from Stripe — a cross-source correlation analysis that quantifies which features actually drive retention, not just which features get the most clicks.

  • You're pre-product-market-fit with <50 customers. If you have 30 customers, you can manually track everything in a spreadsheet. InfiniSynapse's value grows with customer count (200+) and data source count (3+ systems).

If you have Fivetran piping Stripe and product data into Snowflake, dbt models maintaining your SaaS metrics, and a BI tool with dashboards that everyone trusts — InfiniSynapse may overlap with your existing investment. Its strongest value is compressing the path from "data in 3+ silos" to "trusted SaaS metrics" for teams that don't have (and don't want to build) a data engineering function.

FAQ

Can InfiniSynapse connect to Stripe and other billing systems?
Yes. InfiniSynapse connects to your billing system's database (Stripe, Chargebee, Recurly all back onto PostgreSQL or MySQL), your product usage database, your CRM (Salesforce, HubSpot), and your support ticketing system (Zendesk, Intercom). It auto-discovers schemas and joins subscription revenue with product usage data, sales pipeline, and support tickets — all in one query, without ETL or data warehouse modeling.
What SaaS metrics can InfiniSynapse calculate?
Core SaaS metrics include: MRR/ARR (with new, expansion, contraction, and churned breakdown), net revenue retention (NRR), gross and net churn rate, LTV/CAC ratio by cohort and acquisition channel, CAC payback period, expansion revenue rate, feature adoption and DAU/WAU/MAU, conversion rates through PLG funnels (signup → activation → conversion → expansion), and support ticket trends correlated with churn risk. All metrics are defined once in your Knowledge Base and calculated consistently across every analysis — no more "whose numbers are right?" conversations.
How is this different from SaaS BI tools like ChartMogul or Baremetrics?
ChartMogul and Baremetrics specialize in subscription revenue analytics — MRR, churn, LTV — from your billing data. They are excellent at what they do but are limited to billing data. They cannot answer: "Which product features do our highest-NRR customers use that our churned customers didn't?" or "Is there a correlation between support ticket volume in month 1 and churn in month 3?" These questions require joining billing data with product usage data with support data — which is what InfiniSynapse's multi-source federation enables. Think of ChartMogul/Baremetrics as best-in-class for billing-only metrics; InfiniSynapse for cross-source SaaS intelligence.
Can InfiniSynapse handle PLG (product-led growth) metrics?
Yes. PLG metrics require joining product usage data (who signed up, who activated, who hit the paywall) with billing data (who converted, at what plan) with CRM data (which sales-assisted accounts expanded). No single SaaS tool connects all three. InfiniSynapse can analyze the full PLG funnel: signup → activation (reached key milestone within N days) → conversion (free to paid) → expansion (upgraded plan or added seats) — and correlate each stage transition with specific product behaviors, acquisition channels, and account characteristics.
Is our customer and financial data secure?
Yes. InfiniSynapse supports private deployment (Air-Gapped). All billing data, subscription records, customer PII, product usage logs, and support ticket data stay on your own servers, processed locally. Most SaaS analytics tools are SaaS-only and require piping your financial and customer data to external cloud services. Private deployment is essential for SaaS companies subject to SOC 2, GDPR, or enterprise customer data processing agreements (DPAs) that prohibit third-party data access — which includes most B2B SaaS companies above $10M ARR.
