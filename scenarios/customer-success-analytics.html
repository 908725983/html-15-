Three hard problems with siloed customer data

CS teams do not lack data — they lack connected data. These three problems affect CS orgs managing anywhere from 20 to 20,000 accounts:

Problem 1: Customer health lives in five different tools

Product usage is in your production database or product analytics tool. Support tickets are in Zendesk. NPS scores are in a survey tool or spreadsheet. Contract details are in the CRM. Payments are in the billing system. A CSM managing 40 accounts needs to check 5 tools per account — 200 data lookups — just to understand who is healthy and who is not. Hex identifies the core promise: "customer health, built from your relevant data sources" including product usage, billing, support, and CRM. The challenge is that building that unified health score requires data engineering work that most CS teams cannot resource.

Problem 2: Churn signals arrive too late

The typical churn detection workflow: customer stops logging in (but no one notices for 3 weeks because usage data isn't connected to CS workflows), customer files more support tickets (but tickets are in a separate system), customer gives a 5 on NPS (but the survey tool doesn't alert the CSM), customer sends a cancellation email. By the time the CSM is aware, it's too late to intervene. InfiniSynapse can detect the early-warning pattern — declining usage + rising tickets + NPS drop — because it sees all three signals in one place, updated in real time.

Problem 3: Expansion opportunities are invisible until renewal

Which accounts are power users of a feature that's only available in the next tier up? Which accounts have added 50% more seats but haven't been upgraded? Which accounts have high NPS, high usage, and a renewal date in Q3 — making them ideal expansion targets? Answering these questions requires joining usage data, seat counts, NPS, and CRM contract data. Most CS teams discover expansion opportunities during renewal conversations (too late to build the business case) because the cross-source data isn't available day-to-day. Hex frames this as needing to "get ahead of expansion opportunities" — InfiniSynapse makes it possible by connecting the data that reveals them.

Six core customer success analytics workflows

These are the six highest-frequency CS analytics workflows customer success and renewals teams run on InfiniSynapse:

1. Customer health scoring — customer health score

InfiniSynapse computes the score for every account daily, pulling data from all five sources. You can ask: "Show me all accounts with a health score below 50 that are up for renewal in the next 90 days, ranked by ARR."

2. Churn prediction and early warning — churn prediction

InfiniSynapse detects the multi-signal pattern that typically precedes churn: declining weekly active users, increasing support ticket volume (especially for bug-related issues), NPS score dropping 2+ points, and delayed payments. 5. Expansion opportunity detection — expansion opportunities

Ask: "Which accounts have added more than 20% additional seats in the last 6 months but are still on a plan that charges per seat? InfiniSynapse runs this analysis across all accounts continuously, so your CS team never discovers an expansion opportunity after it's too late to act on it.

6. CSM portfolio and workload optimization — CSM portfolio analytics

When InfiniSynapse is not the right choice for customer success analytics

Honest framing — you likely don't need InfiniSynapse if:

  • You already have a CS platform (Gainsight/Totango) and all your data is ingested there. If your product usage data, support tickets, and NPS are already flowing into your CS platform and you're satisfied with its health scoring and analytics capabilities, adding InfiniSynapse may be redundant.
  • Your CS team manages under 50 accounts with straightforward health signals. At small scale, a spreadsheet with manual data pulls may be sufficient. InfiniSynapse's value grows with account volume and data source complexity — when the manual cross-referencing becomes unsustainable.
  • Your churn is driven by product or pricing, not by detectable behavioral signals. If customers churn because of a product gap or competitor pricing rather than declining usage or rising tickets, the cross-source behavioral analysis provides less predictive power.

InfiniSynapse creates the most value when your CS team manages 100+ accounts and needs to join customer data from 4+ systems to understand account health — and when catching churn signals 30 days earlier directly impacts net revenue retention by 5% or more.

FAQ

How does InfiniSynapse calculate customer health scores?
InfiniSynapse computes health scores by joining data from your CRM (account tier, renewal date), product usage database (feature adoption, login frequency, usage trend), support ticket system (ticket volume, severity, resolution time), NPS surveys, and billing data (payment timeliness). You define the formula once in the Knowledge Base, and the AI applies it consistently, refreshing scores on whatever schedule you set.
Can InfiniSynapse predict which customers are likely to churn?
Yes. InfiniSynapse analyzes cross-source signals — declining product usage, increasing support ticket volume, NPS drop, delayed payments — to flag at-risk accounts. It identifies patterns that preceded past churn events and applies those patterns to current accounts. You can set up automated alerts when an account's churn risk exceeds a threshold.
How does InfiniSynapse compare to Gainsight or Totango?
Gainsight and Totango are purpose-built CS platforms with pre-built playbooks, health score templates, and workflow automation. They are strong choices if your data lives primarily in one platform. InfiniSynapse differentiates on data flexibility: it connects to any combination of databases, files, and tools — not just what the CS platform ingests. If you need to join product usage from your production DB, tickets from Zendesk, NPS from SurveyMonkey, and billing from your finance system in one analysis, InfiniSynapse handles the cross-source federation natively.
What is an Account 360 view and how does InfiniSynapse build it?
An Account 360 is a single dashboard showing every critical dimension of a customer: product usage metrics, support ticket history, NPS trend, renewal date, contract value, billing history, key contacts, and health score. InfiniSynapse builds this by joining data across CRM, support, product, NPS, and billing — then presenting a unified view you can query in plain English. Unlike building a 360 in a BI tool (which requires modeling all sources into a warehouse), InfiniSynapse connects directly to each source.
Is my customer data secure?
Yes. InfiniSynapse supports private deployment (Air-Gapped). All customer health data, usage logs, NPS responses, support tickets, and billing information stay on your own servers, processed locally.
